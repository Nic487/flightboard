FlightBoard

Flash your ESP32 directly from the browser
  1. Open this page in Chrome or Edge on a Mac, PC, or Android. Safari and iOS will not work — they don't support Web Serial.
  2. Plug your ESP32 into a USB port using a data-capable cable (cheap charge-only cables won't work).
  3. Click Connect below. A pop-up will ask which serial port to use — pick the one matching your ESP32 (often "USB Serial" or "CP2102" or "CH340").
  4. Click Install FlightBoard. Erase + flash takes about 60 seconds.
  5. When done, unplug and re-plug the ESP32. The OLED shows "WiFi setup needed" and the board creates a FlightBoard-Setup WiFi network. Connect to it from your phone, pick your home WiFi, done.

Driver tip: On macOS Ventura+ and Windows 11, USB-serial drivers are built in for most ESP32 boards. If the port doesn't show up, install the CP210x driver (most common) or the CH340 driver.
